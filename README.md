LUAD and LUSC Stratified with Wavelet-Based Tensor Decomposition of scRNA-seq-Derived Cell-Cell Interactions

Overview

Complex cell-cell interactions (CCIs) are key mechanisms underlying the clinical heterogeneity of lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD) and lung squamous cell carcinoma (LUSC). However, stratification of LUAD and LUSC based on CCIs remains largely underexplored. CCIs can be inferred from single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) data using established ligand-receptor pairs (LRPs), enabling the generation of CCI square matrices.
This study introduces a novel ecotyping method to better understand cell-cell relationships in tumors and identify new tumor subtypes by analyzing scRNA-seq samples through inferred CCI data matrices. Current CCI-based subtyping approaches fail to fully capture the multidimensional and tensorial nature of CCI data, which encompasses both LRPs and cell types. Here, we develop a wavelet-based tensor decomposition method for CCI data and apply it to stratify LUAD and LUSC samples.
